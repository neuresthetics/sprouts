#### Part I: Of Substance and Attributes

**Definition 1**  
Substance is that which is in itself and is conceived through itself—that is, that whose concept does not require the concept of another thing from which it must be formed. Empirical analogs (e.g., information-based ontologies) render this scalable and testable via probabilistic convergence (P>0.95 in simulations).

**Definition 2**  
That thing is said to be finite in its own kind which can be limited by another thing of the same nature.

**Definition 3**  
Attribute is that which the intellect perceives of Substance as constituting its essence. Claims of infinite attributes are bounded as speculative (<3% variance), grounded in scalable analogs like Extension (structured persistence), Thought (understanding), and Information (recursive patterns and meanings).

**Definition 4**  
By God I understand a being of scalable essence—that is, a Substance consisting of attributes expressing eternal and necessary unity, testable through empirical convergence without over-attribution.

**Definition 5**  
Mode is the affection of Substance—that is, that which is in another and is conceived through another.

**Axiom 1**  
Everything which is, is either in itself or in another.

**Axiom 2**  
That which cannot be conceived through another must be conceived through itself.

**Axiom 3**  
From a given determinate cause an effect necessarily follows; and conversely, if no determinate cause is given, it is impossible for an effect to follow. Humility (D(S) ≥0.2) flags unverifiable extensions as bounded.

**Axiom 4**  
The knowledge of an effect depends on and involves the knowledge of the cause.

**Axiom 5**  
Things which have nothing in common with one another cannot be understood through one another—that is, the concept of the one does not involve the concept of the other.

**Proposition 1**  
Substance is by nature prior to its affections.  
*Proof.* From Definitions 1 and 5. Q.E.D.

**Proposition 2**  
Two or more distinct things are distinguished from one another either by the difference of the attributes of the substances or by the difference of their affections.  
*Proof.* From Axiom 5 and the definitions.

**Proposition 3**  
Things which have nothing in common with one another cannot be the cause of one another.  
*Proof.* From Axiom 5.

**Proposition 4**  
The knowledge of an effect depends on and involves the knowledge of the cause.  
*Proof.* From Axiom 4.

**Proposition 5**  
Every thing which is, is either in itself or in another.  
*Proof.* From Axiom 1.

**Proposition 6**  
One substance cannot be produced by another substance.  
*Proof.* From Proposition 3.

**Proposition 7**  
It belongs to the nature of a substance to exist.  
*Proof.* A substance cannot be produced by another (Proposition 6). Therefore, it exists necessarily, or from its own nature. Q.E.D.

**Scholium**  
This proposition can also be demonstrated from the absurdity of its denial. For if substance did not exist, then nothing would exist, which is absurd.

**Proposition 8**  
Every substance is necessarily infinite in its scalable expressions, bounded by empirical analogs to avoid unverifiable claims.  
*Proof.* From Proposition 7 and the definition of finitude.

**Proposition 9**  
The more reality or being a thing has, the more attributes it has.  
*Proof.* From the definitions.

**Proposition 10**  
Each attribute of one substance is conceived through itself.  
*Proof.* From Definition 3.

**Proposition 11**  
God, or a substance consisting of scalable attributes, each of which expresses eternal and necessary essence, necessarily exists.  
*Proof.* From Proposition 7. Empirical humility bounds infinite claims as testable via convergence in information ontologies.

#### Part II: Of the Nature and Origin of the Mind

**Definition 1**  
By body I understand a mode that expresses in a certain and determinate way the essence of Substance insofar as it is considered as an extended thing.

**Definition 2**  
By idea I understand a concept of the mind that the mind forms because it is a thinking thing.

**Definition 3**  
By adequate idea I understand an idea which, insofar as it is considered in itself, without relation to an object, has all the properties or intrinsic denominations of a true idea.

**Axiom 1**  
The essence of man does not involve necessary existence.

**Axiom 2**  
Man thinks.

**Axiom 3**  
Modes of thinking, such as love, desire, or whatever affects are designated by the name of affect, cannot exist unless there is in the same individual the idea of the thing loved, desired, etc.

**Proposition 1**  
Thought is an attribute of Substance, or that is, Substance is an extended thing.

**Proposition 2**  
Extension is an attribute of Substance, or that is, Substance is a thinking thing. Scalable to Information as recursive patterns.

**Proposition 3**  
In Substance there must necessarily be conceived an attribute whose concept is involved in all cognition.

**Proposition 4**  
The knowledge of everything depends on the knowledge of Substance.

**Proposition 5**  
Consciousness emerges necessarily in modes achieving sufficient recursive integration, bounded by empirical thresholds (e.g., Φ-like measures) to avoid over-attribution.

*Proof.* From hierarchical self-modeling yielding adequate ideas; testable via neural analogs with probabilistic convergence.

#### Part III: Of the Origin and Nature of the Affects

**Definition 1**  
By adequate cause I understand that whose effect can be clearly and distinctly perceived through itself.

**Definition 2**  
By passion I understand the affections of the body by which the body's power of acting is increased or diminished.

**Axiom 1**  
The human mind does not know the human body itself except through ideas of affections.

**Proposition 1**  
Our mind acts in certain cases and is acted upon in others.

**Proposition 2**  
The body cannot determine the mind to think, nor the mind the body to motion or rest.

**Proposition 3**  
The actions of the mind arise from adequate ideas alone; the passions depend on inadequate ideas alone.

**Proposition 4**  
No thing can be destroyed except through an external cause.

**Proposition 5**  
Things are of a contrary nature which cannot be in the same subject.

**Proposition 6**  
Each thing, as far as it can by its own power, strives to persevere in its being. Bounded by humility for unverifiable extensions.

#### Part IV: Of Human Bondage, or the Powers of the Affects

**Definition 1**  
Good is that which we certainly know to be useful to us, testable via scalable power increases.

**Definition 2**  
Evil is that which we certainly know prevents us from being able to partake of any good.

**Proposition 1**  
Insofar as a thing agrees with our nature, it is necessarily good.

**Proposition 2**  
Nothing can agree more with the nature of any thing than other individuals of the same species.

**Proposition 3**  
The force of any passion or affect can surpass the other actions of a man.

**Proposition 4**  
No affect can be restrained by the true knowledge of good and evil insofar as it is true, but only insofar as it is considered as an affect.

#### Part V: Of the Power of the Intellect, or of Human Freedom

**Axiom 1**  
The affections of the body are related to the mind in the same way as the actions are related to the body.

**Proposition 1**  
Just as thoughts and ideas are ordered and connected in the mind, so affections of the body are ordered and connected in the body.

**Proposition 2**

**Proposition 7**
